M O U N T A B U

Dilwara Temples or Delvada Temples are a group of Jain temples located about 2.5 kilometers from the Mount Abu settlement in Sirohi District, Rajasthan’s only hill station 1. The temples were built between the 11th and 16th centuries and are famous for their use of white marble and intricate marble carvings 1. The five Dilwara Temples are among the most famous Jain temples 1. The Vimal Vasahi is much the earliest, constructed by 1031, with the Luna Vasahi by 1230, and the others at intervals between 1459 and 1582 1. All are in a very white marble that adds greatly to their effect, and remain in use 1. The oldest and largest two have large amounts of intricate carving even by the standards of the style, reaching a peak in the Luna Vasahi temple 1. The main buildings of the first three named are surrounded by “cloister” screens of devakulikā shrines, and are fairly plain on the outer walls of these; in the case of the Vimal Vasahi this screen was a later addition, around the time of the second temple .
